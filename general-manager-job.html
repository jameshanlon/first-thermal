First Thermal

General Manager Vacancy

Job Title / Position: General Manager, First Thermal Ltd. approx. 27-month contract until end December 2023, with opportunity for extension. Probationary period 3 months.

Reporting to: MD/CEO

Based: Bristol

If you would like a rewarding and stimulating job doing something you can be proud of, this is a unique opportunity to develop First Thermal Ltd. (FTL) a new social enterprise company, set up to be a social franchisor. FTL has been formed to build franchises that replicate the Bristol-based multiple-award-winning C.H.E.E.S.E. Project CIC, (TCP) which delivers thermal imaging Energy Tracing® surveys to reduce energy loss from homes - thus making warmer homes and a cooler planet. You will be joining a team of directors who have successfully developed the project over five years.

This exciting and important environmental and social development is possible thanks to a grant from Energy for Tomorrow - a Centrica Initiative.

Your Role - developing the Franchise

To support the board of directors to develop and adapt the business model of TCP to become the profitable exemplar for the new FTL Franchise business. Overseeing the implementation of a franchise manual, bespoke IT booking system, and unique equipment and training to create a streamlined efficient service, allowing franchisees to generate sustainable trading income.

In the first 12-15 months you will oversee the recruitment of franchise pilots to test and adapt the business plan and franchise model, working with pilots to achieve a trading surplus and the highest possible quality franchise and customer experience, to enable expansion of the network nationwide from 2023. You will progressively take over the management from the MD/CEO, and on achieving financial stability by December 2023, there is an opportunity to continue and grow with the company.

If you do not already have experience of managing franchises, it will be necessary to qualify in the British Franchise Association's Qualified Franchise Professional course (which will be funded by FTL - about 8 days.)

Building a team

Your team includes expert consultancy support (in place) a 0.6FTE Franchise Development and Marketing Manager- to be recruited, and an Office Assistant/PA, shared with the MD/CEO. (On account of his age, the MD/CEO wishes to gradually withdraw to an Executive Chairman role.) TCP will recruit its own Survey Manager, developing to Manager of the franchise, and an expanding team of part-time Energy Tracers® trained by FTL.

Finance

  • Develop business strategy and business plan to sustainable profit, setting meaningful KPIs in collaboration with the Board to drive all aspects of First Thermal's operations.
  • Report back to our grant funders, Energy for Tomorrow, on achievement of grant contract milestones to secure 6-month funding tranches.
  • Together with our external accountants, oversee production of accounts and writing annual reports thereon.
  • Identify, develop bids and secure further funding sources.

Sales and Customer Service

  • Responsible for engaging new franchisees assisted by Franchise Marketing/development Manager (to be recruited October).
  • Overseeing development of TCP customer base with TCP survey manager.
  • Assist franchisees in developing their customer base.
  • Create a culture of customer service according to the First Thermal standards, ensuring the best possible customer experience.
  • Be responsible for delivering our aims of Warmer Homes and a Cooler Planet.

Strategic partnerships and development

  • Work with the directors to amplify constructive relationships in the local, regional and national energy sector, to strengthen brand and business partnerships and to deliver low-cost targeted retrofitting opportunities; including with: Councils, Housing Associations, Architects, conservation organizations and regional and Westminster government.
  • Develop partnerships with large commercial retrofit businesses, to work with local franchises.
  • Work with directors and staff developing new analyses of survey outcomes as carbon savings, etc.
  • Assist with development of new processes and equipment from Energy Tracers CIC and Heatview - both belonging to directors of FTL.

Project Management

  • Manage deliverables necessary for launching pilot and succeeding franchises, including Franchise Manual and equipment; our bespoke Heatview® thermal cameras and Energy Tracing® Blower doors.
  • Assist with development of, set up, and oversee training of Energy Tracers® (ET's) for TCP and franchisees, including production of training documents, and CPD certification.

Line Management

  • To provide strong management, including planning, recruitment, training, motivation and team building in line with First Thermal policies, values and standards.
  • To regularly evaluate the performance of all team members, including ETs and franchisees and provide regular feedback to ensure results.
  • Administer the collection and confidential archiving of survey results on building performance begun by TCP, extended to franchisees.
  • Administer the company Pension scheme.

Operations

  • Conduct ongoing appraisals with all franchisees agreeing commercial development objectives.
  • Conduct regular franchisee visits to ensure all franchisee Energy Tracing® carried out to the highest possible standard.s
  • Ensure all health and safety, safeguarding and other company policies are maintained and any issues are resolved in a timely manner.
  • Secure good commercial awareness of activities of competitors within local and national trading environments.
  • Additional responsibilities may be added according to the instructions of the Board.

The C.H.E.E.S.E. Project CIC (Cold Homes Energy Efficiency Survey Experts)

To work with the MD/CEO and TCP Survey Manager, becoming line manager, until the Survey Manager (to be recruited in September) takes over as Franchise Manager for TCP.

  • To develop contacts and contracts with Bristol City Council, WECA, City Leap contractors, Housing Associations, etc.
  • To recruit as partners further anchor community organizations thus sustaining community links.
  • To monitor survey numbers and performance of ETs.
  • To sustain existing relationships with NGOs as members of TCP: Bristol Energy Network, Centre for Sustainable Energy, CHAS, Re:work, Bristol Green Capital, etc.
  • Recruiting/developing further members and partnerships with: Age UK, We Care, Citizens Advice, Shelter etc.
